# Benzodiazepine and Z-drug Prescribing Across NHS Scotland
An independent analysis of benzodiazepine and z-drug prescribing patterns across all 14 NHS Scotland health boards, using WHO Defined Daily Dose (DDD) methodology and Public Health Scotland open prescribing data (2024–2025).
Key findings:

Prescribing rates vary more than two-fold across health boards — from 8.2 DDDs/1,000/day (Orkney) to 18.2 (Lanarkshire)
Rates are falling across all boards but the geographic gap persists
Street benzodiazepine deaths correlate with prescribing rates; prescribed benzodiazepine deaths do not — suggesting two distinct harm pathways

Data sources: Public Health Scotland Prescriptions in the Community; National Records of Scotland Drug-related Deaths 2024; NRS Mid-year Population Estimates
